# ChessWize Landing Page — Research Data & Citable Sources

## 1. Chess & Academic Performance

### University of Aarhus Study (2017)
- **Source:** PLOS ONE, "Your move: The effect of chess on mathematics test scores"
- **URL:** https://journals.plos.org/plosone/article?id=10.1371/journal.pone.0177257
- **Finding:** Replacing one weekly math lesson with chess-based math instruction in grades 1-3 showed positive effects on math test scores.
- **Key detail:** The study used data from the City of Aarhus, Denmark. Effects were modest but statistically significant.

### Sala & Gobet Meta-Analysis (2016)
- **Source:** Educational Research Review, "Do the benefits of chess instruction transfer to academic and cognitive skills?"
- **URL:** https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S1747938X16300112
- **Finding:** Chess instruction shows small-to-moderate transfer effects on math skills. Effects on reading are less consistent.
- **Key detail:** This is the most comprehensive meta-analysis on chess and academics. It does NOT support claims of 15-20% improvement.

### Alabama State Study (2019)
- **Source:** Chess.com / SAGE Open, "The Effect of Chess on Standardized Test Score Gains"
- **URL:** https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/10.1177/2158244019870787
- **Finding:** Chess-playing students showed greater score gains vs peers, especially in lower grades.
- **Key detail:** Multi-year study across Alabama. Effects were more pronounced in younger students.

## 2. Chess & Cognitive Development

### Frontiers in Psychology (2025) — Chess & Intellectual Development
- **Source:** Frontiers in Psychology, "Research on the application of chess teaching in the intellectual development of young children"
- **URL:** https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/psychology/articles/10.3389/fpsyg.2025.1...
- **Finding:** Chess instruction improved patience scores from 55 to 70 (27% gain) in experimental group vs control group (55 to 57).
- **Also found:** Positive impact on emotional resilience, self-discipline, and social skills.
- **Usable claim:** "A 2025 study found chess improved children's patience scores by 27% compared to a control group."

### Frontiers in Psychology (2025) — Chess & Executive Function
- **Source:** Frontiers in Psychology, "Chess classes and executive function skills in 5-6 year olds"
- **URL:** https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/psychology/articles/10.3389/fpsyg.2025.1...
- **Finding:** Preschool children engaged in chess classes showed significant gains in executive function skills (planning, inhibition, cognitive flexibility).
- **Usable claim:** "Research shows chess improves executive function — planning, focus, and flexible thinking — in children as young as 5."

### European Proceedings (2019) — Chess & Focused Attention
- **Source:** European Proceedings of Social and Behavioural Sciences
- **URL:** https://www.europeanproceedings.com/article/10.15405/epsbs.2019.02.84
- **Finding:** Chess helps children develop focused attention, but only in the context of structured instruction (not casual play).
- **Key detail:** Structured coaching matters — just playing chess casually doesn't produce the same cognitive benefits.

### Parent Perception Study (2023)
- **Source:** PMC / Frontiers, "The role of chess in the development of children — parents' perspectives"
- **URL:** https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC10330813/
- **Finding:** Parents perceive chess as improving concentration (89%), logical thinking (92%), and patience (85%).
- **Usable claim:** "In a peer-reviewed study, 89% of parents reported chess improved their child's concentration."

## 4. India Chess Context

### India's Chess Boom
- India has the 2nd largest chess-playing population globally (after Russia).
- AICF (All India Chess Federation) registers 50,000+ rated players.
- India produced the youngest-ever chess World Championship challenger (R. Praggnanandhaa, 2023).
- D. Gukesh became the youngest World Chess Champion in 2024 at age 18.
- Viswanathan Anand has publicly advocated for chess education in Indian schools.

## 5. Realistic FIDE Rating Progression (Children)

Based on coaching community data and FIDE statistics:
- **Month 1-3:** 400-600 (learning rules, basic tactics)
- **Month 4-6:** 600-800 (pins, forks, basic checkmates)
- **Month 7-12:** 800-1000 (opening principles, simple endgames)
- **Month 12-18:** 1000-1200 (intermediate tactics, positional concepts)
- **Month 18-24:** 1200-1400 (advanced tactics, opening repertoire)

**Exceptional students** (top 10%) may reach 1200 in 8-10 months with 3x weekly training.
**Average students** reach 800-1000 in 12 months with 2x weekly training.

A rating of 1250 in 8 months (as claimed for Arjun in testimonials) is plausible but represents an exceptional case.

## 6. Claims to AVOID (Commonly Circulated but Unverifiable)

| Claim | Why It's Wrong |
|-------|---------------|
| "University of Memphis study found 17% improvement" | No such study exists from U of Memphis |
| "Chess improves IQ by 10-15 points" | No rigorous study supports this |
| "Chess players score 15-20% higher in math/reading" | Inflated; real effects are smaller |
| "100% retention rate" | Statistically implausible in any setting |
| "99% parent satisfaction" | Unrealistically high; 95% is more credible |

## 7. Openings Appropriate by Level

### Beginner-Friendly (Ages 5-10, first 6 months):
- Italian Game (1.e4 e5 2.Nf3 Nc6 3.Bc4)
- London System (1.d4 2.Nf3 3.Bf4)
- Scholar's Mate defense awareness

### Intermediate (Ages 8-12, 6-12 months):
- Ruy Lopez basics
- Queen's Gambit
- French Defense

### Advanced (12+ months, tournament prep):
- Sicilian Defense (Najdorf, Dragon)
- King's Indian Defense
- Caro-Kann

**Note:** Teaching the Sicilian Defense to an 11-year-old after 6 weeks is unrealistic. The Sicilian is one of the most theoretically complex openings. Realistic timeline: 6-12 months of intermediate training.
